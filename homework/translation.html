Happy Sachiko

I will introduce you to an urban legend and a spell that is passed down in Tenjincho, a local city in a certain prefecture. Until about 30 years ago, Tenjin Elementary School actually existed in this town. There was a rumor that Sachiko, a student who went missing here long ago, was still walking around the school building after her disappearance. Sachiko is already dead, and she wears bright red clothes, pale skin, and long hair. If you meet her at school, she will grant your worries and wishes, such as unrequited love or friends. This legend is the origin of "Sachiko of Happiness." The elementary school where the rumor started has now been demolished (a junior high and high school has been built on the site), and it has faded away to the point that even the local people don't know about it, but I have investigated and unearthed the details. This spell is meant to strengthen the bond between friends and vow to stay together as lifelong friends. It may be a good idea to do this before a sudden farewell, such as graduation or transfer.

[Happy Sachiko]

Number of people: 2 to 9 or 10 people
Required items: Sachiko spirit summoning model (download from here → ★ ), scissors, cutter, etc.

[Caution]
  • Be careful when handling sharp objects to avoid injury.
  • Always remember to respect Sachiko's spirit.
[Procedure]
  1. Download → Carefully cut out the printed out model with scissors or something similar to create a Sachiko doll.
  2. Grab any part of the finished doll with the friend you want to bond with for life.
  3. Check how many people will be performing the spell. After that, everyone closes their eyes and silently chants "Sachiko, please" in their minds the number of times equal to the number of people in the group.
    Note at this time! When chanting in your mind, do not repeat yourself even if you make a mistake. Absolutely do not make a mistake in the number of chants. It is said that Sachiko will get angry if you fail.
  4. Once you've finished chanting, grab the doll firmly with your claws and pull it apart.
  5. Please take good care of the pieces of the doll you cut off and put them in your wallet or student ID holder so they don't get lost.
